## Install MetaMask and create an Ethereum account

In order to interact with a smart contract on Ethereum, you will need to install a wallet and initialize an account with some ether in it. 

MetaMask, one of the most popular wallet apps, has an extension that can be installed directly into your web browser as well as a mobile app that you can use on iOS and Android. [Download MetaMask here](https://metamask.io/download.html) and create a new wallet.

 ### ‚ùóÔ∏è
 
 Your wallet credentials should be kept private!
 
 When creating your new MetaMask wallet, you'll be given a seed phrase of 12 randomly generated words. These words can give you (or anyone else!) access to your account to send transactions and add or remove funds from your account. Keep this seed phrase somewhere secure, and never share it on the internet. We recommend [1Password](https://1password.com/) for keeping it secure.

## Add some Rinkeby ether from a faucet to your account

Once you are finished with setting up your MetaMask account you will see a brand new account with 0 ETH in it ready for you to use. Switch over from the Ethereum mainnet to the Rinkeby testnet by clicking on the network names at the top and selecting Rinkeby, which is the default Ethereum network used on our [testnet website](http://testnets.opensea.io/) 

![965](/images/e1218e8-mm-os-2.png "mm-os-2.png") ![941](/images/f4b7e6b-mm-os-3.png "mm-os-3.png") 

To add some Rinkeby ether to your wallet, you can go to the [Rinkeby Faucet](https://faucet.rinkeby.io/) and request some Rinkeby ether be added to your account. Note that you might have to verify a non-malicious request for ether by posting on social media.

Alternatively, download this starter project with some preset dependencies.
